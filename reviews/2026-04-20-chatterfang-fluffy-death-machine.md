## The "Plan A" Audit (Strategic Summary)

* **The engine:** Create token volume, convert bodies into mana/cards/drain, and use Chatterfang to control creature combat math.
* **The spice:** Squirrel conversion turns generic token cards into removal and aristocrat pressure at the same time.
* **Tactical vulnerabilities:** Graveyard suppression, repeated board wipes, and stax pieces that shut off sacrifice outlets.

## Interaction & Protection Quality

* **Stack interaction density:** Limited in Golgari, so proactive disruption and sequencing matter.
* **Board wipe asymmetry:** Strong when death triggers and token rebuild are online.
* **Engine protection:** Protect key outlet plus token-engine permanents before committing all resources.

## Turns 1 to 4 Goldfish Simulation

Assumptions: on the play, no opposing interaction.

### Hand A (Ideal)

* **T1:** Land plus one-mana acceleration or token setup.
* **T2:** Cast Chatterfang or secondary engine piece.
* **T3:** Add token producer and hold removal activation line.
* **T4:** Convert tokens into board control and chip/drain pressure.

### Hand B (Grinder)

* **T1-T2:** Two lands plus incremental ramp or draw setup.
* **T3:** Commander with no immediate payoff, pass with interaction.
* **T4:** Engine online, start accruing sacrifice value.

### Hand C (Mulligan test)

* **Opening:** Two lands, high curve, no ramp.
* **Line:** Mulligan in most bracket-4 pods; keep only with early draw support.

## Recommended Play Lines & Piloting

* **Opening hands:** Keep hands with acceleration plus one token engine or draw engine.
* **Deployment timing:** Deploy Chatterfang when you can generate immediate token value the same turn.
* **Closing the game:** Prioritize drain or overrun lines that punish one wipe-resistant board cycle.
